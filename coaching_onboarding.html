Dedicated Agents: AI Training Coach (Trainer-Agent) & Financial Operations Agent (Finance-Agent)

The Trainer-Agent manages automated tutorial sequences inside the CRM dashboard. The Finance-Agent audits transaction ledgers, matches client subscription states, and automatically logs accounts receivable.

1. Enabling Scale Through Stability

The Coaching & Support team represents the long-term retention value of Project D. While the Growth team starts the fire and the Tech team builds the engine, you ensure the fire keeps burning and the engine is mastered by the client. We do this by helping their staff master their private AMP Center Dashboard, training them to treat it as their internal assistant agentic workforce.

The Support Philosophy: We do not just "answer questions." We elevate proficiency. We show client staff how the unique combination of agentic workflows and CRM UI keeps them organized and efficient, replacing legacy manual tasks.

2. AI Staff Trainer Playbook

Training is not a one-time event; it is an ongoing coaching relationship. Your mission is to ensure the client's staff uses the AI agents we provide to actively increase their operational capacity. Your primary workspaces are Neural Chat (utilizing the simulator console) and the Tactical Calendar (scheduling client workshops).

How to Conduct Simulator Training (Neural Chat Console)

Your primary coaching tool is the AI Automations Simulator Console inside the client's CRM sandbox Neural Chat interface:

  • Run Trigger Simulations: Walk client staff through simulations (e.g. *Botox Reactivation*, *6 AM Standby Broadcast*, or *Fleet Damage Ticketing*).
  • Audit the Outputs: Show them how to audit the Terminal Logs panel in real-time as the simulator executes. This verifies agent actions visually.
  • SMS Verification: Demonstrate how keyword replies (like `BOOK`, `TIRE`, or rating stars) trigger automated messages on the mock phone screen and execute CRM Kanban board movement.

Adoption Audits & Tactical Calendar Scheduling

  • Calendar Management: Coordinate and schedule all staff onboarding webinars and monthly check-ins on the Tactical Calendar.
  • Check Login Logs: Audit weekly login frequencies inside the database. If user activity metrics drop, proactively schedule follow-up reviews.
  • Gamify Results: Review dashboard stats (e.g. *Total Active Leads*, *Automated Bookings*, *Hours Saved / Week*). Call out and praise team members who achieve high numbers.

The Goal: By month 3, the client's staff should feel like they are "Project D Certified", confidently managing their own CRM pipelines and tuning automation filters.

3. Operations & Finance Standards

You manage the administrative and financial functions that allow the CEO and COO to focus on aggressive growth. Your work is the surgical back-end of the agency, primarily owning and utilizing the MRR Tracker.

  • Financial Precision (MRR Tracker): Manage client contracts, monthly subscription plans, and process manual payouts inside the MRR Tracker ledger. Invoices should be generated automatically when a client card is moved to Closed Won on the Leads Board.
  • SOP Stewardship: Maintain and update our internal Standard Operating Procedures (SOPs). As the agency evolves, you ensure our "How-To" guides evolve with us.
  • Admin Logistics: Support hiring, contract management, and internal scheduling. You are the one who ensures the Lead Developer and CEO have a clean path to their next Discovery session.

4. The Project D Calm

In a fast-paced agency, the Coaching & Support team must be the voice of calm and reliability:

  • Responsiveness: Aim for same-day resolution for internal admin and client support requests.
  • Transparency: Keep the COO updated on all financial and adoption metrics weekly.
  • The "Steady" Tone: Whether you are coaching a nervous staff member or chasing an invoice, remain professional, helpful, and firm.
