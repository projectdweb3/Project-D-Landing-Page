1. The AI-First Developer & The AMP Center

At Project D, we do not code line-by-line in isolated terminal windows. We **orchestrate architectures** directly within the **AMP Center** platform. The AMP Center is our primary **assistant agentic workforce**—replacing standalone coding tools (like Antigravity or Claude Code) by combining complex agentic pipelines with an organized, visual CRM UI. As a developer, your job is to build custom agent sandboxes and map API gateways using our integrated workspace:

CRM-Integrated Agent Workspace

The AMP Center acts as the central coordinator. Rather than managing raw agent outputs via CLIs, developers deploy specialized agents straight to client Kanban boards, where users see, track, and manage their executions visually.

Custom Agent Sandboxes

We co-engineer custom CRM views, database schema, and webhook configurations for each enterprise customer, wrapping raw agentic power inside a structured, user-friendly control center.

Technical Standard: If a task takes you more than 15 minutes of manual script writing, you aren't leveraging the AMP Center's developer-agent capabilities. Your role is to configure hooks, test pathways, and design workflows that keep the agentic workforce organized and efficient.

2. The Lead Dev's Secret Weapon

The Lead App & Web Developer sits in on Discovery calls to perform what we call **"Real-Time Architecture"**:

The "Wow" Moment Execution

  • 01. Listen: During the first 15 minutes of the call, identify the prospect's visual taste, colors, and operational bottlenecks (e.g. driver callouts or slow lead responder times).
  • 02. Build Live: While they are talking, use the AMP Center sandbox environment to quickly provision a fresh database instance and scaffold a high-end landing page configured with their brand colors and logo.
  • 03. The Reveal: Share your screen. Showcase the live landing page and the CRM Leads Board containing their brand information. Demonstrate that we can build in 15 minutes what other agencies pitch in 3 weeks, proving the visual organizational power of our CRM UI.

3. Customizing the AMP Center CRM

Developers are responsible for scripting webhook triggers and gateway endpoints inside the client's sandbox code:

1. Webhook Scripting

Configure script listeners to process incoming payload variables (like SMS text messages or form submissions):

  • Script conditional statements to parse text keywords (e.g. `BOOK`, `TIRE`, `GLASS`).
  • Connect API responses to execute database state updates (e.g., changing lead stages to **Closed Won** or **Demo Scheduled**).

2. Workflow Connections

Link automated triggers to transactional events, allowing agents to coordinate tasks:

  • Sick Callout Webhook: Triggers SMS blasts to standby driver arrays.
  • OCR Background Check: Parses driver licenses and logs database validations automatically.
  • POS Integration: Listens for checkout payloads to execute Review Booster texts.

4. Junior Developer Training

As a Junior Developer, you manage the **Rapid Implementation** phase (Phase 03) to deliver client dashboard custom configurations:

  • CRM Integration: Master the Twilio and Email SMTP gateway setup process. You are responsible for ensuring the AMP Center connects perfectly on every client domain.
  • Widget Configuration: Embed reservation calendars and review sliders into client web domains linked to their new agents.
  • Database Setup: Populate the initial contacts lists and configure user access controls.
